What's new?

"1984" by George Orwell is a dystopian novel published in 1949 that explores the dangers of totalitarianism. Set in the fictional state of Oceania, it follows Winston Smith, a man living under the oppressive rule of an authoritarian regime led by the figurehead Big Brother. The Party, the ruling power, exerts complete control over every aspect of life, including thoughts, history, and even language. Through constant surveillance and propaganda, the Party manipulates reality and crushes dissent, leaving individuals powerless to resist. As Winston secretly rebels against this regime, the story delves into themes of freedom, individuality, truth, and the consequences of unchecked political power.
